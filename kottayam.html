KOTTAYAM

Kottayam is a city in the Indian state of Kerala and the administrative capital of the Kottayam district. It is located in central Kerala, bordered by the Western Ghats to the east and the Vembanad Lake and Kuttanad paddy fields to the west.

Kottayam is a city in the Indian state of Kerala, flanked by the Western Ghats on the east and the Vembanad Lake and paddy fields of Kuttanad on the west. It is the district headquarters of Kottayam district, located in south-west Kerala.

Most of India's natural rubber originates from the acres of well-kept plantations of Kottayam, also home to the Rubber Board. Kottayam is also called as “Akshara Nagari” which means the “city of letters” considering its contribution to print media and literature.
